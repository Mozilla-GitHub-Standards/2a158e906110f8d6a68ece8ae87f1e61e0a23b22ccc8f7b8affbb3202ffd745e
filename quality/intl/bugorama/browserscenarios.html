International Bugorama

Thanx for attending the International Bugorama. Our primary objective is to discover bugs, problems, or design flaws that might hinder International users from using Mozilla. For this reason, you should pick a language besides English for all your testing. If possible, use a machine with an non-English operating system. If you don't understand a language other than English, you can still play the game. Just use the translate feature from the view menu.
 

Special International Features

If you haven't had any international user experience, you will get to play with a couple of cool features that may be new to you:
  1. View | Character Coding submenu. This submenu lets you change character coding on any page at any time. Although many web pages indicate their character coding in HTML, many others do not. If you come accross a page that looks like garbage, try changing the character coding.
  2. View | Translate dialog. This feature presents you with a list of languages you can translate into or from. Highly recommended as light entertainment, as well as an invaluable aid for getting the "gist" of a document.
  3. Edit | preferences | fonts. This preferences panel lists the fonts on your system and (hypothetically) permits you to change fonts. Don't be surprised if it isn't ready to go yet.

Machine locations

If you already have a machine running an international operating system, please use that.
 

Your Mission (should you decide to accept it)

Congratulations! You have just been hired by agents of the Evil Empire to find the secret (or obvious) failings of Mozilla browser. Your spy managers will be known to you only as amasri@netscape.com and roberts@netscape.com. As always, should any of your activities be discovered by enemy agents, the secretariat will disavow all knowledge. Also, if you buy anything online, you will have to pay for your own purchases! Your mission is to go undercover in the country of your choice, using the language of that country, and assume one of the following cover identities. Notice that these scenarios are open-ended: you won't be able to do everything. Just use the scenarios as a starting point for exploring seamonkey in your own, unique way.

CEO of a startup company

As CEO, you have several pressing needs that can be met over the internet. As CEO, you know best what these are, but the secretariat would suggest:
  1. Figure out just exactly what your company does. If you can't figure out anything better, you may assume that your company sells Martian Real Estate, so the only expenses you will have are paper (for printing the deeds) and attorneys (to defend yourself against fraud claims)
  2. Research companies with similar products or services. If you are selling Martian Real Estate, you might investigate software companies that specialize in vaporware.
  3. Find employees to fill key positions
  4. Find government functionaries who may be helpful to you
  5. Rent office space and buy equipment.
  6. Buy secret spy stuff
  7. Put together an AIM buddy list with all your top executives

Winner of "Do you want to be a millionaire"

As winner or $1,000,000, you need to find stuff to buy with your all money. You may want to find some good investments (if you haven't spent all your money yet). You could look for retirement homes or tax havens. Be sure to use wallet--it makes spending money so much easier. If you don't want to pay the IRS, you could also look for a good tax attorney. Be sure to use the wallet for filling out forms, and remember, the secretariat will not be signing any expense vouchers!
 

Reporting Results

Once you have finished your mission, report your findings, on paper or email, to amasri@netscape.com or momoi@netscape.com.
