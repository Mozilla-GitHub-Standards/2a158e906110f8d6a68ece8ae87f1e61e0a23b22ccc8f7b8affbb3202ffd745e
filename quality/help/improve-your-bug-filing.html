Improving Your Bug Filing

Hi. Thanks for joining us. If you're committed to improving your bug filing, we are committed to providing resources to make it as easy as possible for you to do so. Here's the four stage plan for better bugs.

Before thinking about filing:

  • Wait a week. Seriously. If you aren't one of the people who hangs out on IRC and does smoketesting, then you are very unlikely to be the first person to file obvious bugs such as "mail doesn't load". Most regressions which have been noticed are fixed inside a week; download a build a week later and, if the problem is still there, start the process of reporting it.
  • Remember that most big requests for enhancement have already had bugs filed on them. For years. You won't be the first to suggest that Mozilla should support PGP.

Before filing:

  • Always check the Most Frequently Reported Bugs list. There is no excuse for filing a duplicate of a bug listed here.
  • Search to see if the bug has already been reported. Yes, the Bugzilla search form is complicated - that's because it's powerful. Read this tutorial and learn to use that power, and you'll save yourself and the QA team a lot of wasted effort.
  • Read the Bug Writing Guidelines. This is an extremely important document, with many years of distilled wisdom in it. Read, learn and inwardly digest.

When filing:

  • Use the "guided" bug submission form, which codifies the Guidelines. Using it all the time is a good way to make sure you don't accidentally miss out any of the important details. Fill in all the standard fields, even if they impart trivial information.
  • Think about it from an engineer's point of view. When they read your bug report, what will their reaction be? "Yes, I understand the problem exactly, and I might even know what the cause is" or "I have no clue what broke, when and how"? If they'd have an obvious question, answer it in the initial report.

After filing:

  • Respond promptly to any questions posted in the bug; they will arrive via email, and you should visit the URL of the bug to respond to them. (Do not mail anyone directly.)
  • If your bug ends up resolved as a duplicate, invalid or worksforme, try and work out why. Did you not find the duplicate in your searches? Why not? Maybe it needs a better summary. Or, if it was marked worksforme and yet you still see the problem, what extra information can you give to enable someone else to reproduce it?
