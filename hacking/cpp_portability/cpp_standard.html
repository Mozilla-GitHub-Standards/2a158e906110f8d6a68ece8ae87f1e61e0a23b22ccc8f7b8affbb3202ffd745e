the C++ standard

You want to write good C++ in general... Specifically, you'll need some on-site guidance to help you:

  • a language lawyer to help you avoid the dark corners
  • a copy of the C++ standard
  • exemplary documentation on good (and portable) coding practices, such as Meyers' trilogy of ``Effective'' books
  • human readable documentation on the language, such as The C++ Programming Language (3rd ed)
  • care about warnings, fix them as soon as possible; really care about warnings from your best compiler, even when it's not your favorite compiler

resources

  • Effective C++: 50 Specific Ways to Improve Your Programs and Designs (2nd ed)
  • More Effective C++: 35 New Ways to Improve Your Programs and Designs
  • More Effective STL: 50 Specific Ways to Improve Your Use of the Standard Template Library
  • The C++ Programming Language (3rd ed) also describes the standard library, you may prefer the hardbound special edition
  • ISO/IEC 14882-1998 (the C++ standard, as a PDF file, for US$18)
