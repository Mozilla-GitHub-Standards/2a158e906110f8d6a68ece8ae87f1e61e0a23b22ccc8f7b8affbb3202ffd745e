This document keeps track of the ongoing localization efforts for the Mozilla Application Suite program. On this page you can find:

  • all the registered localization projects, their contact information,
  • and the contributed works.

We also host localization efforts for other mozilla.org products.

Mozilla Application Suite Localization Projects

Below are listed the registered localization projects and the contact information for their teams. Currently 115 projects registered, plus English (US).

All the listed contributors are volunteers, willing to make Mozilla available in their own language. If your language is not listed, or you think the current registered people need further help (the rightmost columns show the last localized releases), you can learn how to localize Mozilla, contact the already registered contributors, and register yourself as contributor either through the newsgroup, or contacting the MLP Staff.

Localization Contributions

Localized builds provide the binaries compiled for a particular platform, and entirely substitute the English distributions provided by mozilla.org.
The lang packs constitutes a much smaller download, which are automatically retrieved from the net and installed on your local Mozilla application. Once installed, you can switch between the current interface language and the installed one using the Appearance | Languages/Content panel in the Preferences dialog (Edit | Preferences).

If you wish to enable a non English build, to have English (US) as selectable language for its interface, choose the proper version of your Mozilla copy: The table below shows all localization contributions for recent Mozilla releases (v1.4 release and later). You can also find the material for versions from 1.0 till 1.6, and from 1.0 Release Candidates till older Milestones (M8).
This material has been provided by our contributors.
