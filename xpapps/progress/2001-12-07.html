Navigator Team Progress for the week ending 12/7/ 2001

Highlights

  • I'm very pleased to announce that Jan Varga has joined our merry band of developers.  Jan has been doing great work on mozilla for quite a while now, most recently  by implementing a content model view for <outliner> last week. 
  • Dave Hyatt added support for specifying multiple border colors and moz-image-region in CSS, and added an  nsITheme API to GFX for code-level rendering of widgets (and a prototype that can paint a Windows XP button).  These three items were the infrastructure needed for a simplification of themes that should significantly speed up the chrome!
  • Joe Hewitt built on Dave's work by  simplifying most of the modern theme, and has so far measured an 18% new window savings and 500k of footprint reduction!
  • Blake Ross checked in bookrmarksliner, which sped up bookmarks and fixed more bugs than even he expected.  He also fixed several other performance and footprint bugs.
  • Bradley Baetz split html dir listings up into several tables to improve performance.
  • The Navigator team resolved 246 Bugzilla bugs, fixing 75 of these.  
  • We have begun using the nsbeta1 nomination/triage model, joining the rest of the apps teams in this.  If you know of a bug assigned to any of us that you think should be fixed for MachV, please nominate it by adding the nsbeta1 keyword.  

Lowlights

  • Paul Chen, a battle scarred veteran of the browser wars, is finishing his second tour of duty at Netscape on Wednesday.  We'll miss him, and wish him all the best in the future.
  • Probably still have too many bugs targetted for 0.9.7 ( 44 in bugzilla ).  
  • Still digging out from under our untargetted bugs, 257 in Bugzilla. If we can just get the show-stoppers nominated for mozilla1.0 and nsbeta1,  our triage team should be able to whittle that list down to size.
