Tab Browsing

Tab Browsing is a revolutionary enhancement to the way you surf the web. While it might seem like just another variant of multiple windows, it streamlines how you view and navigate web pages. Basically you can open any link in a new tab, which by default open behind the current page, so that you keep reading what you're currently looking at while they load.

 

 

By clicking on any of the links on the page with your middle mouse button, or by holding down the Ctrl key while clicking with the left mouse button, you cause that link to open in a background tab. If you were reading a page such as the forum above, or a news site, you could graze through the index page, opening links that interest you in background tabs, and then look at them at your leisure, without having to go back and forward!

 

Stop Waiting, Start Surfing

While you're browsing the index page, the pages you're about to view are loaded in the background, so that when you switch to them they've usually completely loaded, making internet load times melt away. Tab browsing reduces the time you spend waiting for things to happen.
