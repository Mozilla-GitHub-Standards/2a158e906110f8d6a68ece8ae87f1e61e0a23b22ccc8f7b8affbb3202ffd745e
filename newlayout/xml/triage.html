FAQ:

1) What is a targeted bug?

A targeted bug is one that

  1. is futured (has a target milestone of "Future"), or
  2. is PDT approved (marked nsbeta2+, nsbeta3+, or rtm+ in the status whiteboard), or
  3. is awaiting PDT approval for a milestone (marked nsbeta2, nsbeta3, or rtm in the keywords field but does not have a corresponding PDT +/- registered in the status whiteboard field)
2) What is an untargeted bug?

An untargeted bug is one that

  1. is not futured and does not contain a nsbeta2 or nsbeta3 or rtm keyword (Query 1), or
  2. was marked nsbeta2- by the PDT, but does not contain a nsbeta3 or rtm recommendation (Query 2), or
  3. was marked nsbeta3- by the PDT, but does not contain a rtm recommendation (Query 3), or
  4. was marked rtm- by the PDT, but has not been futured (Query 4).
3) When I come across an untargeted bug, what do I do?

Use your judgement to decide whether this should be futured or fixed in beta 2, beta 3, or rtm.  If you want to future the bug, mark it "Future" in the target milestone field.  If you want to recommend it for beta 2, beta3, or rtm, mark it nsbeta2, nsbeta3, or rtm in the keywords field.
