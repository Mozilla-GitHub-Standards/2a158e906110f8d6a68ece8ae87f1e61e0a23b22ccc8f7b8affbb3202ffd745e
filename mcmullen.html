June 23, 1999

mozilla.org is deeply saddened to report the passing of John McMullen, Netscape employee and valued member of the Mozilla community. Please watch this page for more information as it becomes available.

Memorial Service for John

Don Melton and I just visited with John's widow, Jane, and had a good talk. She is bearing up well under the strain, and is grateful for all of the attention she is receiving from family, friends and co-workers. She has made arrangements for a memorial service to be held at Spangler's Mortuary, 799 Castro St., Mountain View, California, on Tuesday, June 29, at 4:00 PM. She hopes that those of us who knew John will come and talk about the happy memories of good times we had with him. She also mentioned that tulips were John's favorite flowers.

Peter Trudelle

Message from Bob Lisbonne

From: Bob Lisbonne
To: Netscape
Date: Wed, 23 Jun 1999 16:58:28 -0700
Subject: John McMullen

Folks,

Regrettably, we learned earlier today that John McMullen had passed away. John was an engineer in CPD, and helped lead our Macintosh development on Communicator 4.0, 4.5, and 5.0. Before any of that, he earned a PhD in pure mathematics; lectured at universities on three continents; learned French, German, Italian, and Russian; and was an avid musician and composer.

Each of us will deal with this tragic loss in our own way. For those who would appreciate the chance to talk about it with a trained professional, I'd recommend contacting our Employee Assistance Program at 888-265-1782. They are available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

Netscape is doing all the right things, and is helping John's wife make appropriate arrangements. As details become available, they will be posted to http://www.mozilla.org/mcmullen.html.

I know that John's friends and colleagues send to his family their condolences, thoughts, and prayers. We'll miss him.

Sincerely,

--Bob Lisbonne
Sr VP, Client Products Development
Netscape Communications
